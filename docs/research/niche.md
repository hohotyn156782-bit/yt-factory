## 4. Recommendation

### #1 — History documentaries & historical biographies (long-form, 20–45 min, docu-style)
Reasoning: (a) education-band RPM $8–14 with story-driven watch time that multiplies it; (b) the *only* niche where every constraint is a strength — Internet Archive/Prelinger footage, public-domain paintings/photos, FLUX+Ken Burns recreations are the *native* visual language of history docs; (c) all subjects are dead → zero defamation risk; (d) fully evergreen search demand; (e) LLMs write narrative history better than any other genre; (f) proven by channels monetized from an April 2025 start. **The one condition:** do NOT clone the "boring history sleep slop" template — that sub-niche is flooded and is the exact target of 2026 inauthentic-content enforcement. Differentiate like Fern: researched original scripts with cited sources, chaptered editing, consistent branded narrator voice, human QC pass, disclosure checkbox on. Skew topics toward **wealth/business/engineering history** to pull premium advertisers without YMYL advice.

### #2 — Space/science long-form explainers
NASA public domain gives unlimited legally-bulletproof cinematic visuals; evergreen; lower RPM ($4–10) but the 40+ minute "space documentary to fall asleep to" format compounds watch time and overlaps the ~$10.92 sleep/ambient RPM band. Ideal second channel reusing the same pipeline.
